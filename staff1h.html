Research

Research interests

I am interested in the use of molecular tools to understand processes that maintain and erode genetic diversity in natural populations. I am also interested in the application of these tools to solve ecological problems of importance for conservation and management.

My previous research has involved assessing genetic diversity, gene-flow and population structure in declining populations of bumblebee species. I have also developed molecular tools to identify cryptic species of bumblebee and to identify wireworms (which are significant agricultural pests of potato). In addition to work on insects, I have also been involved in a large-scale research project to help understand the marine ecology of Atlantic salmon (the ‘SALSEA-Merge’ project).

More recently, I have become interested in the nature of the relationship between estimates of genetic diversity based on neutral markers and those based on functional regions: it is important to understand how adaptively important genetic variation is affected by the forces known to shape neutral genetic variation and the consequences for conservation. Recent work has focussed on identifying patterns of selection at potential candidate loci involved in immunity and metabolism in bumblebees within this context.

Current projects include:

  • Conservation genetics of the Hazel dormouse with Dr Ed Harris and Fraser Combe (MMU PhD student)
  • Adaptive conservation genetics of Bombus terrestris with Dr Mairi Knight (Plymouth University) and Prof. Dave Goulson (University of Stirling)
  • Adaptive conservation genomics of European Grayling with Dr Martin Taylor (UEA), Dr Robin Sen (MMU) and the Grayling Research Trust.

The beauty of molecular tools is that they can be broadly applied across a wide range of taxa. If you have research interests on any group that you feel match my interests I am very happy to discuss potential PhD studentship applications, post-doctoral research or other collaborations.

Current Research Group

Vanessa Huml, PhD student

Vanessa is funded by Manchester Metropolitan University (where I used to work) and the Grayling Research Trust, but is based in Plymouth. She is examining genetic diversity of MHC loci in European grayling (Thymallus thymallus) in relation to bacterial and environmental parameters as well as in contrast to neutral variation, using next-generation sequencing approaches (Ion Torrent). Collaborating on the project are Dr Ed Harris and Dr Robin Sen (MMU), Dr Martin Taylor (UEA), Richard Cove (Natural Resources Wales). Dr Steven Weiss is an external advisor.

http://www.sste.mmu.ac.uk/research_students/staffbiog/default.asp?StaffID=1013

Kirsty Lloyd, PhD student

I co-supervise Kirsty with Dr Mairi Knight (Plymouth Uniiversity, DoS), Dr Mark Brown (Royal Holloway) and Prof Dave Goulson (Sussex). Kirsty is examining the population genetics of the colonisation and

subsequent expansion of Bombus hypnorum in the UK.

http://www.plymouth.ac.uk/staff/klloyd1

Sarah Rustage, PhD student

I co-supervise Sarah with Dr Mairi Knight (Plymouth Unversity, DoS) and Dr Mark Brown (Royal Holloway). Sarah is near the end of her PhD examining genetic diversity and fitness (parasite load and immunocompetence) in Bombus monticola (in contrast to the closely related but widespread B. pratorum).

http://www.plymouth.ac.uk/staff/srustage#

Fraser Combe, PhD student

Fraser is studying for his PhD at Manchester Metropolitan under Dr Ed Harris (DoS). Fraser is examining landscape and conservation genetics of the hazel dormouse (Muscardinus avellanarius) in the UK. He works closely with a number of wildlife trusts, but particularly with Dr Simone Bullion at Suffolk Wildlife Trust.
